Hi, I have been awfully quiet here for a while now. And that's because all my attention has been going into building up a business as independent developer.
I am developing an open source data visualization tool called [Shaper](https://github.com/taleshape-com/shaper) and offer paid hosting and support [services](https://taleshape.com/plans-and-pricing/) for it.

## Why Shaper?

This all started 16 months ago when I was consulting a company that wanted to setup a data stack and build embedded, **customer-facing analytics dashboards** into their product. Initially I built a solution that combined various existing open source tools, but the result felt **too complex and hard to maintain**.

I was wishing for a simpler tool that does it all in one. And I naively thought "how hard can it be?" and set out to build a tool that is simple and works for simple use cases. I saw an opportunity to simplify dashboard building compared to traditional business intelligence software by using **DuckDB as in-process query engine** to query across various data sources, and by using **AI to write SQL** instead of building a custom dashboard builder UI.

And with this Shaper was born as the **"BI as SQL - powered by DuckDB"** solution.

## Current Status

That initial consulting client turn into my first customer so from the beginning I was building Shaper **driven by needs of actual users**. And half a year later I was lucky that another company I work with in the past was also interested in this embedded analytics solution and after a **proof of concept** they quickly became my second customer. So my work kept being driven by customer feedback and real use cases.

Then eventually I had to get the word out and start **finding customers** beyond the lucky start. So I open sourced Shaper, built a website, documentation, started blogging and getting the word out. I saw clear interest in the open source tool I built and Shaper is now actively used by more companies than I even know about.

_At this point I can say without a doubt that **Shaper provides real value** to users._

But I also learned that interest in the open source product does not automatically translate to interest in my paid services. So while I keep improving the product, I am now learning how to **build a sustainable business** around it.
